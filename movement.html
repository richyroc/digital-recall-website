Memory and Movement

In this documentary, Tom O'Donnell, a dancer and choreographer gives us an insight into his use of technology to augment the memory of his routines.

Director: Laura Ni Dhubhghaill
Camera Operators: Melanie Rabier, Laura Ni Dhubhghaill, Austen Donohoe, Cormac O'Brien, Richard O'Connell
Music: Matt Hill (PRS) – April Shower
Special Thanks to Tom O'Donnell, George Griffin

Extras available with this video: multi-angle video of Tom's solo dance.

Read more about the concept >>

Director's Statement:

Have you ever watched a video of yourself doing something? Has it ever changed your opinion of the event? This documentary follows one dancer who records himself at home every day in order to perfect his dancing skills. This compelling story of one man's dedication really struck a chord with our group focus – memory and technology.

Tom O'Donnell has only been dancing for two years, but one would think he had been dancing forever. He uses home videos that he records of himself to improve and alter his own memory of his routines. In our interview with Tom, we were allowed real insight into one man's use of technology to support his own recollections.

Laura Ni Dhubhghaill
